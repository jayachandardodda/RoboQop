Portfolio Optimization

Portfolio optimization is a process of constructing an investment portfolio that aims to maximize returns while minimizing risks. It involves selecting a mix of assets such as stocks, bonds, commodities, and others, based on various factors including historical performance, expected returns, volatility, correlation, and investor preferences. The goal is to achieve the optimal balance between risk and reward, considering an investor's specific objectives and constraints. Here are some key concepts and techniques related to portfolio optimization: 1. Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT): Developed by Harry Markowitz, MPT is a framework for portfolio construction that considers the trade-off between risk and return. It suggests that by diversifying investments across different assets with uncorrelated or negatively correlated returns, an investor can reduce the overall risk of the portfolio without sacrificing returns. 2. Efficient Frontier: The efficient frontier represents a set of optimal portfolios that offer the highest expected return for a given level of risk or the lowest risk for a given level of return. It helps investors visualize the possible combinations of assets that provide the most efficient risk-return trade-offs.
